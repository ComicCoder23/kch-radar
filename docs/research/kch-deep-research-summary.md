# KCH Deep Research Summary

## Executive Summary
Kirkintilloch (KCH) is a place of deep layers, not just a series of events. Its history spans from the Roman Antonine Wall to the medieval burgh, industrial canal, and foundry heritage. Its current community infrastructure is dense, including support services, volunteer networks, and local civic-cultural anchors. KCH is positioned to be a trusted local knowledge layer rather than a thin directory.

## Strongest Findings
- **Roman & Industrial Layers**: The Antonine Wall and the Peel Park archaeological core provide a unique heritage foundation. The canal, marina, and Lion Foundry history offer strong, visual place-based storytelling.
- **Civic Hubs**: The Auld Kirk Museum, Town Hall, and William Patrick Library form an unusually strong civic-cultural core.
- **Community Infrastructure**: Beyond events, there is a deep layer of fellowship (Men’s Shed, lunch groups), support (Foodbank, Carers Link, recovery services), and youth/family programming.
- **Archive Layer**: The EDLC Archives & Local Studies service, alongside the National Library of Scotland's digital guides, provides an essential, rich documentary record.

## Missing Layers Identified
- **Archive Layer**: Initial KCH planning lacked access to archival records (court books, historic plans, newspapers, film).
- **Community Infrastructure**: The breadth of volunteer, support, and peer-fellowship services was underrepresented.
- **Recurring Programme**: Focus was too narrow on "big events" rather than the weekly rhythms (Bookbug, film, clubs) that build local life.
- **Surrounding Settlements**: A rigid town-only boundary misses essential connections to Lenzie, Twechar, and neighbouring settlements.
- **Freshness/Trust**: Without explicit states for active/stale/closed items, local trust is at risk.

## Local Opportunity Areas
- **Heritage Trails**: Use the Roman-to-industrial corridor to create human-centred, carefully curated heritage guides.
- **Volunteer Routes**: Use existing volunteer infrastructure to bridge the gap between "need help" and "community help."
- **Local Guides**: Develop thematic guides (e.g., "Kids/Rainy-day," "Heritage/Walks," "Joinable/Fellowship") based on the high-value clusterings identified.

## Privacy & Safety Warnings
- **Safeguarding**: Avoid scraping private groups or personal data. Only use official public-facing contact information.
- **Data Integrity**: Implement a "Trust & Freshness" model. Do not list businesses or groups without monthly or quarterly verification status.
- **Support Sensitivity**: Handle recovery, foodbank, and support listings with care, using only official public descriptions.

## Verification Requirements
- **Business Audit**: Verify status of local independent businesses (some listings identified may be stale).
- **Recurring Activity**: Confirm current status of pub/club/hall-based event series.
- **Infrastructure Check**: Confirm accessibility and current opening status for all core venues mentioned.
